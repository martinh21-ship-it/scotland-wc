How it works. Each press of Run simulation plays out every unplayed match 20,000 times using team-strength estimates, then ranks the twelve third-place teams exactly as FIFA does (points → goal difference → goals scored). The gauge shows how often Scotland land in the top eight. Lock in real scores as they happen and the unplayed games shrink, so the number gets sharper as the tournament goes on.

Reading it. If Scotland finish 1st or 2nd in Group C they qualify outright (counted as 100%). Otherwise it's the third-place race, where the margin of any defeat matters enormously. Strength ratings are approximate — treat the figure as a well-grounded estimate, not a precise probability.
